Signals

Identifying whether or not a customer is potentially abusive is the first step involved in the CAP process. This identification is done on the basis of signals - assigned to a customer's order at the time of order placement, they help identify orders placed by potentially abusive customers.

This signal assignment is done in one of 3 ways: Via ML Models, CS Owned Heuristic rules or evidence based models

Systems

At the start of 2022, all CAP order flagging was SYSKA based. Currently, we're tracking an effort to onboard the Concession Reduction Engine (CRE) to make all our heuristic rule flagging real time (in tandem with the ML base flagging).
The CRE system aims to reduce concession leakage by:

  • Deprecate syskas and o to a banner based flagging of orders (within 1-2 hours of the order being placed)
  • Primary Workflow suppression - Primary is not allowed to handle the concession workflow for Flagged orders.
  • Auto routing - routed basis banner + customer intent on filling the start contact form.[24% in Dec ‘22]
  • All signals consolidate in the CRE (Concession Reduction Engine). Post the consolidation, on the basis of Risk band the final signals are assigned (a higher risk band = a riskier customer)
  • ORC Reason codes are not linked to foresight reason codes {END of Q1]
